# First Scenario Bible: Gate of Ash

## Scenario Identity

- Pack title: Black Stela - Gate of Ash
- Town: Stela Gate Town
- Dungeon: The Ashen Descent
- Core mystery: the black stela is not a monument above the dungeon; the dungeon
  is the wound around its buried root.
- Player promise: map carefully, retreat before pride gets expensive, and bring
  proof home instead of letting the dungeon narrate victory for the party.

## Reference Boundary

Classic DRPGs are references for structure only: attrition, mapping tension,
town recovery, party roles, dangerous knowledge, and fair secrets. Black Stela
does not copy maps, names, monster identities, puzzles, prose, or proprietary
progression from those works.

## Floor Arc

| Floor | Role | Beat |
| --- | --- | --- |
| B1F - Silent Approach | Onboarding | Learn movement, map reading, trap, combat, and safe return. |
| B2F - Split Dust | Branching attrition | Choose routes, find a shortcut, feel first resource pressure. |
| B3F - Cistern Teeth | Resource pressure | Optional treasure and the first status-oriented threat. |
| B4F - Turned Lanterns | Navigation twist | Read one-way and dark-zone clues without unfair guessing. |
| B5F - Toll of Cinders | Midpoint gate | Miniboss, shortcut unlock, and town preparation pressure. |
| B6F - Narrow Oaths | Deep route | Stronger traps and party role checks. |
| B7F - Side Ash Vaults | Optional risk | Rare reward behind a keyed side objective. |
| B8F - Gate of Ash | Finale | Boss pressure, proof reward, and return-path test. |

## Enemy Families

- Ash Slime: weak attrition and tutorial pressure.
- Hook Rat: ambusher that punishes careless branching.
- Bitter Mote: first status-style threat for recovery pressure.
- Lantern Ward: blocker that slows navigation floors.
- Cinder Keeper: midpoint miniboss and preparation check.
- Oath Cutter: deep-route ambusher.
- Vault Husk: optional side objective blocker.
- Ash Votary: finale boss for the first scenario pass.

## Item and Reward Tone

- Consumables should make another room or two possible, not erase risk.
- Equipment rewards should change preparation decisions but stay small enough
  that party knowledge matters more than raw stats.
- Rare rewards should invite a return trip instead of demanding full completion
  on first contact.

## Japanese Naming Notes

- Keep "Black Stela" as the product name.
- Use 黒碑 for in-world stela references where a compact Japanese term helps.
- Prefer plain, readable floor names over ornate fantasy compounds.
- Avoid naming that implies the game controls player speech, feelings, or
  decisions.
